# Usage Guide

A walkthrough of Joules' features and how to use them day-to-day.

---

## First-Time Setup

After creating your account, the onboarding wizard collects:

- **Height and weight** — used for TDEE calculation
- **Age and sex** — used in the Mifflin-St Jeor formula
- **Activity level** — sedentary, lightly active, moderately active, very active, or extra active
- **Diet goal** — see [Diet Plans](#diet-plans) below

This information sets your daily calorie and macro targets. You can update it anytime in **Settings → Profile**.

---

## Logging Meals

### Photo Logging (AI)

1. Tap the **+** button on the dashboard or Log page
2. Select **Photo**
3. Take a photo or upload one from your library
4. Optionally add a description (e.g. "large portion" or "half serving") — this improves accuracy
5. The AI identifies each food item and estimates calories and macros
6. Review and confirm — you can edit individual items before saving

**Tips for better AI results:**
- Good lighting makes a big difference
- Include the whole plate in frame
- If there's a nutrition label visible, the AI reads it directly (most accurate)
- Add a hint like "restaurant serving" or "home cooked" to help the AI calibrate portions

### Manual Logging

1. Tap **+** → **Manual Entry**
2. Enter the food name, calories, and optionally protein/carbs/fat
3. Save

---

## Dashboard

The dashboard shows today's summary:

- **Calorie ring** — consumed vs. target (net of exercise if applicable)
- **Macro bars** — protein, carbs, fat
- **Water intake** — progress toward your daily water goal
- **Meals today** — list of everything logged

### Quick Logging from Dashboard

The dashboard has quick-add buttons for water and exercise so you don't need to navigate away.

---

## Water Tracking

Log water intake with the water button. You can log in milliliters or oz depending on your unit preference (set in **Settings → Units**).

Your daily water goal is calculated based on body weight (roughly 35ml per kg, adjusted for activity level).

---

## Exercise Logging

1. Go to **Log → Exercise** or use the quick-add on the dashboard
2. Select an exercise type (running, cycling, strength training, etc.)
3. Enter duration in minutes
4. Calories burned are estimated using MET values

Burned calories are subtracted from your net calorie target for the day.

---

## Weight Tracking

Log your weight in **Log → Weight**. The **Progress** page shows a chart of your weight history over time.

Weigh yourself consistently — same time of day, same conditions — for the most useful data.

---

## Diet Plans

Joules supports 8 diet plans:

| Plan | What it does |
|------|-------------|
| **Calorie Deficit** | Standard deficit below TDEE for weight loss |
| **Keto** | Very low carbs (<50g/day), high fat |
| **Intermittent Fasting 16:8** | Eating window: 8 hours |
| **Intermittent Fasting 18:6** | Eating window: 6 hours |
| **Intermittent Fasting 20:4** | Eating window: 4 hours |
| **OMAD** | One meal a day |
| **Paleo** | Focus on whole foods; no grains, dairy, or processed foods |
| **Mediterranean** | High in vegetables, fish, olive oil, whole grains |

Switch plans anytime in **Settings → Diet Plan**.

---

## AI Health Coach

The **Coach** tab gives you two things:

1. **Daily tips** — personalized advice based on your current diet plan and recent activity, refreshed daily
2. **Chat** — ask any question about nutrition, your logs, exercise, or diet strategy

The coach has context about your profile and recent food logs, so it can give specific rather than generic advice.

Examples of things to ask:
- "Am I getting enough protein today?"
- "What's a good keto snack under 200 calories?"
- "How many days until I hit my weight goal at this rate?"

---

## Achievements

Joules unlocks achievement badges for milestones like:

- Logging your first meal
- Maintaining a streak of days within your calorie target
- Hitting your protein goal multiple days in a row
- Reaching weight loss milestones

View them on the **Achievements** page.

---

## Installing as a Mobile App (PWA)

Joules is a Progressive Web App — you can install it on your phone's home screen for a native app feel.

**iOS (Safari):**
1. Open Joules in Safari
2. Tap the Share button
3. Tap **Add to Home Screen**

**Android (Chrome):**
1. Open Joules in Chrome
2. Tap the menu (three dots)
3. Tap **Add to Home screen** or **Install app**

Once installed, it works offline for viewing past logs.

---

## Exporting Your Data

Go to **Settings → Export** to download your data as CSV files. Available exports:

- Meals log
- Weight history
- Water log
- Exercise log

---

## Units

Switch between metric and imperial in **Settings → Units**:

- **Height**: cm or ft + in
- **Weight**: kg or lbs
- **Energy**: kcal or kJ

The database always stores metric values; the app converts for display.
